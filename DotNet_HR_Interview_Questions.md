# .NET Interview -- Common HR & Behavioral Questions

## 1. Tell me about yourself

**What they're evaluating** - Communication skills - Relevant
experience - Confidence - Career direction

**Recommended structure** 1. Present role/background 2. Relevant
experience 3. Technical skills 4. Why you're interested in the role

**Example answer**

> I'm a software engineer with experience in C#, .NET, ASP.NET Core, SQL
> Server, and REST APIs. I began by working on embedded systems and IoT
> projects, which strengthened my programming and problem-solving
> skills. Over time, I transitioned into backend development, where I've
> built web APIs, worked with databases, and learned software
> engineering best practices.
>
> Recently, I've also been working with Docker, Linux, Git, and testing
> frameworks while continuing to improve my .NET knowledge.
>
> I'm now looking for a role where I can contribute to real-world
> products while continuing to grow as a backend developer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Why do you want to work here?

**They're evaluating** - Interest in the company - Preparation -
Long-term motivation

**Example**

> I like that your company works on modern .NET technologies and
> emphasizes engineering quality. I'm looking for an environment where I
> can learn from experienced developers, contribute to meaningful
> projects, and continue growing technically.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Why are you leaving your current job?

**Example**

> I've learned a lot in my current role, but I'm looking for
> opportunities that offer more backend development, larger-scale
> applications, and stronger career growth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Why should we hire you?

**Example**

> I bring strong programming fundamentals, practical .NET experience,
> and a willingness to learn quickly. I enjoy solving problems, writing
> clean code, and adapting to new technologies. I'm confident I can
> contribute while continuing to grow with the team.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. What are your strengths?

**Example**

> One of my strengths is problem-solving. I enjoy breaking down complex
> issues and debugging them systematically.
>
> Another strength is my willingness to learn. Recently I've expanded my
> skills by working with Docker, Linux, and automated testing alongside
> .NET development.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. What are your weaknesses?

**Example**

> Earlier in my career I sometimes spent too much time perfecting
> implementation details. I've improved by prioritizing tasks better and
> focusing on delivering working solutions before refining them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Tell me about a challenge you faced.

**Use the STAR method** - Situation - Task - Action - Result

**Example**

> During a project, our Docker-based environment wasn't working because
> of permission issues on Linux. I investigated the problem, learned
> about Docker daemon permissions, configured the correct user group,
> verified the setup, and documented the solution.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Tell me about a mistake you made.

**Example**

> I once overlooked a configuration issue because I assumed the code was
> at fault. After spending time debugging, I realized the problem was in
> the configuration. Since then I always verify environment and
> configuration settings before investigating application logic.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?

**Example**

> I'd like to become a strong backend engineer with deep expertise in
> .NET, cloud technologies, distributed systems, and software
> architecture. I also hope to mentor junior developers and take
> ownership of larger projects.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. Why .NET?

**Example**

> I enjoy working with C# because of its clean syntax, strong tooling,
> and object-oriented features. The .NET ecosystem is mature, performs
> well, supports cross-platform development, and is widely used for
> enterprise applications.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 11. Why should we choose you over other candidates?

**Example**

> While there may be candidates with more experience, I believe my
> strong fundamentals, practical project experience, eagerness to learn,
> and ability to adapt quickly make me someone who can contribute
> effectively and continue growing with the team.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 12. How do you handle pressure?

**Example**

> I stay organized by breaking work into smaller tasks, prioritizing
> based on deadlines, and communicating early if I encounter blockers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 13. Describe your work style.

**Example**

> I like to understand requirements thoroughly before implementation,
> write clean and maintainable code, test carefully, and communicate
> regularly with the team.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 14. Do you prefer working alone or in a team?

**Example**

> I'm comfortable with both. I enjoy collaboration because it leads to
> better solutions and faster learning, but I'm also capable of
> independently taking ownership of tasks.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 15. How do you learn new technologies?

**Example**

> I usually start with official documentation, build a small project,
> and then deepen my understanding through practice and real-world
> examples.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 16. Are you willing to relocate?

**Example**

> Yes, I'm open to relocation for the right opportunity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 17. What motivates you?

**Example**

> I enjoy solving challenging problems, learning new technologies, and
> seeing software that I've built being used by real users.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 18. What are your salary expectations?

**Flexible answer**

> I'm looking for a competitive package that is in line with the
> responsibilities of the role and the market. I'm open to discussing
> the complete compensation package.

**If a number is required**

> Based on my experience and market rates, I'm expecting around ₹8--10
> LPA, though I'm open to discussion depending on the overall
> opportunity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 19. Why is there a gap in your resume?

**Example**

> During that period, I focused on improving my technical skills by
> working on personal projects, learning Docker, Linux, and advanced
> .NET concepts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 20. Are you interviewing elsewhere?

**Example**

> Yes, I'm exploring a few opportunities that align with my background,
> but I'm looking for a role where I can contribute meaningfully and
> continue growing over the long term.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 21. Do you have any questions for us?

**Good questions** - How is success measured for this role in the first
six months? - What does a typical day look like? - What technologies
does the team use besides .NET? - How are code reviews conducted? - How
large is the engineering team? - What learning opportunities are
available? - What technical challenges is the team currently solving? -
What are the next steps in the interview process?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 22. Is there anything else you'd like us to know?

**Example**

> I'm someone who enjoys learning continuously and taking ownership of
> my work. I'm excited about opportunities where I can contribute to
> building reliable software while growing as an engineer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# General Interview Tips

-   Keep answers between 1--2 minutes.
-   Support answers with real examples.
-   Stay positive about previous employers.
-   If you don't know something, explain how you'd find the answer.
-   Be ready to discuss every project on your résumé.
-   Practice answering aloud to improve confidence.
